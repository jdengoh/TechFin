# TechFin — Pitch Outline & Script

> **Format**: 5–7 minute pitch + Q&A
> **Tone**: Confident, story-driven, conversational. Speak to judges, not at them.
> **Rule**: Slides are backdrops. The story is in the room.

---

## Slide 1 — Hook (30 seconds)

### Slide Content
> **ONE BIG LINE, bold, centered:**
> *"The last time retail investors had an edge over Wall Street... they don't."*
>
> Sub-line (smaller): *What if you could know the world before the market does?*

### What to Say
> "Raise your hand if you've ever bought a stock — and then watched it drop the next morning because of a news event you didn't see coming."
>
> *(pause for effect)*
>
> "That's not bad luck. That's an information gap. And that gap has always existed between retail investors and institutional traders. Until now."

**Presenter tip**: Make eye contact with two or three judges. Pause after the question. Let the silence land.

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem (45 seconds)

### Slide Content
> **Three bullet points, large font, minimal text:**
> - Bloomberg Terminal costs **$24,000/year**
> - Retail investors react to news — institutions *predict* it
> - Geopolitical events move markets. Nobody connects the dots for you.
>
> Small visual: newspaper headline on left → stock chart crashing on right (cause → effect)

### What to Say
> "Wall Street traders have entire research teams monitoring geopolitical events — trade wars, central bank announcements, M&A rumors — and mapping them to specific stocks before the market opens."
>
> "Retail investors? We read Reddit. We scroll Twitter. We hope."
>
> "The tools that solve this problem — Bloomberg, AlphaSense, Refinitiv — cost $15,000 to $24,000 per year. That's not for retail. That's never been for retail."
>
> "So there's a $4.5 billion market of people who are flying completely blind. And that is the problem TechFin solves."

## Slide 3 — The Solution (60 seconds)

### Slide Content
> **Product name + tagline, centered:**
> ## TechFin
> *Know the world before the market does.*
>
> Three icons in a row (simple, visual):
> 1. 🌍 **World Events** → detected in real time
> 2. 🔗 **Connected to Sectors & Stocks** → via AI knowledge graph
> 3. 💬 **Explained by your AI analyst** → ask anything, get grounded answers

### What to Say
> "TechFin is Bloomberg intelligence for the price of Netflix."
>
> "Here's how it works in three steps."
>
> "First — we aggregate news from Yahoo Finance, Reddit, Twitter, LinkedIn. Every hour. Automatically."
>
> "Second — instead of just showing you articles, we build a **knowledge graph** that maps relationships: which geopolitical event links to which sector, which sector connects to which stocks in *your* portfolio. This is the core of our tech — and it's genuinely different from anything else in the retail market."
>
> "Third — you can ask our AI chatbot anything. 'What's happening with NVDA?' 'How does the US-China trade policy affect my holdings?' And unlike ChatGPT, it doesn't hallucinate. It queries the graph. It shows you real, structured data."

**Presenter tip**: Use your hands to show the three steps. Keep it physical and energetic. Do NOT read from the slide.

### Common Judge Questions — Prep Answers

**"Isn't this just a Bloomberg competitor?"**
> "No — Bloomberg targets institutions at $24K/year. We target the 150 million retail investors who will never afford Bloomberg. Different customer, different price, different architecture."

**"How do you handle financial advice regulations?"**
> "TechFin is an intelligence tool, not a licensed advisor. We frame all outputs as informational insights, not personalized financial advice — the same model Seeking Alpha and Morningstar use. As we scale, we'll engage with the MAS FinTech Regulatory Sandbox."

**"What stops Bloomberg from building this?"**
> "They could — but they won't, because retail investors are not their market. And even if they did, our knowledge graph architecture takes years to build. The data moat deepens with every article ingested."

**"How do you make money before you hit scale?"**
> "Our CAC is $15 via community growth — no paid ads. Even at 200 Pro subscribers in month 6, we're at $45K ARR with minimal burn. We're capital-efficient by design."

**"What's the data quality like without real API keys?"**
> "The product always renders — we built graceful mock fallbacks throughout. With real keys (RapidAPI, Reddit OAuth), it's live. The graph ingestion pipeline runs hourly via APScheduler. Data freshness is a function of our API tier, not our architecture."
